Transnational Transformative Justice Roundtable

Transnational Transformative Justice Roundtable at the 2019 National Women's Studies Association Conference in San Francisco, California. A written, expanded version of this roundtable will be published in a new volume on abolition feminism from Haymarket Books in 2021.

Erica Meiners (U.S. / Northeastern Illinois Univ, Sister Jean Hughes Adult High School) Shirley Leslie (U.S. / Critical Resistance) Mimi Kim (U.S. / Cal State Long Beach, INCITE! & Creative Interventions) Kelsey Mohamed (UK / Cradle Community, Community Action on Prison Expansion) Molly Rosabelle Ackhurst (UK / Birkbeck Univ. of London, Hollaback London) Vanessa E. Thompson (Germany / Goethe University Frankfurt, Copwatch_ffm) Melanie Brazzell (Germany & U.S. / UC Santa Barbara, What really makes us safe? Project) Lauren V. Caulfield (Australia / Abolitionist & Transformative Justice Center) Anne-lise Ah-fat (Australia / Undercurrent Community Education Project)

This roundtable brings together activists & scholars from various national contexts from the Global North working on abolition and transformative justice, particularly in the fields of sexual, interpersonal, and gender-based violence. The roundtable explores how these transformative justice practitioners navigate the carceral state, as well as carceral feminism and queer punitivity, in their local and national contexts. The roundtable invites panelists and the audience to share skills and stories about community-based approaches to violence. What commonalities and differences show up in international comparison when we map how transformative justice is being applied in different communities?
